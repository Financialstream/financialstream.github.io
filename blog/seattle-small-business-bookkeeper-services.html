What business owners usually need first

Most requests start with one of three scenarios: cleanup of prior months, steady monthly bookkeeping, or support before tax return preparation. If payroll and sales tax are involved, those workflows need to be aligned with bookkeeping from day one.

What to prepare before your first bookkeeping consultation

Prepare your entity type (LLC/S-corp/sole proprietor), state(s) of operation, accounting software status, and whether books are current. It helps to list active bank and credit card accounts, payroll platform, and whether you file sales tax monthly or quarterly.

How QuickBooks bookkeeping should connect to payroll and sales tax

Monthly categorization alone is not enough. Reliable books require reconciliations, consistent coding rules, and period-close checks. Payroll liabilities and sales tax balances should match reports, not be adjusted blindly before deadlines.

Why this matters before tax season

When records are clean monthly, tax return preparation is faster, with fewer correction cycles. When records are fragmented, tax prep becomes expensive and risky because core numbers are still being rebuilt during filing season.
