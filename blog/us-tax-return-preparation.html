U.S. tax return preparation services: what to gather before you start

Updated: March 2026

← Back to Updates

Tax return preparation moves faster when the records are complete before review begins. Whether the return is for an individual, a sole proprietor, or a small business, the first step is always the same: gather the documents that explain the full picture.

Tax return preparation in the U.S.: start with the basic tax packet

Collect identification details, prior-year returns, income forms, business summaries if applicable, and any notices that may affect the current filing. If something changed during the year, such as moving states, starting a business, or changing filing status, that should be flagged early.

For small business tax return preparation, keep bookkeeping and tax prep connected

Tax preparation becomes much smoother when bookkeeping is current. Clean monthly records reduce follow-up questions, help support deductions with better documentation, and make the final review more efficient.

If the books are behind, it is usually better to identify that before filing work begins rather than after the review is already underway.

Documents that commonly delay the process

Missing statements, incomplete payroll summaries, unanswered notices, and unclear owner transactions are common reasons tax preparation slows down. If any of those exist, mention them up front instead of waiting for the review stage.

How the preparation process usually works

Most engagements follow a simple structure: gather documents, review completeness, clarify questions, prepare the return, and then complete the final filing or delivery steps. The cleaner the first document package, the smoother each later step becomes.
