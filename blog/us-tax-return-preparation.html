U.S. tax return preparation: what to gather before you start

Updated: March 2026

Back to Updates

This article is written for English-language search intent around U.S. tax return preparation, tax return preparation services, tax filing checklist, and documents needed for tax return preparation. The goal is simple: help you gather the right information before review starts so the filing process moves with fewer delays.

Start with the basic tax packet

Most tax return preparation projects begin with the same foundation: prior-year returns, current-year income forms, identification details, and a short explanation of your situation. If something changed during the year, such as moving states, starting a business, changing filing status, or receiving notices, that should be flagged early.

That first packet matters because it shapes the scope of the work. A clear starting set of documents makes it much easier to confirm what is missing and what should be reviewed first.

When tax return preparation usually takes longer

Preparation usually takes longer when there are several income sources, multiple states, contractor income, late bookkeeping, payroll issues, or unresolved notices. In those cases, the filing is only one part of the job. The bigger task is often cleaning up the underlying records so the return can be prepared accurately.

If that applies to your situation, it helps to say so at the start instead of waiting until the review is already in progress.

For small business tax return preparation, keep bookkeeping and tax prep connected

Tax preparation becomes much smoother when bookkeeping is current. Clean monthly records reduce follow-up questions, help support deductions with better documentation, and make the final review more efficient.

If the books are behind, it is usually better to identify that before filing work begins rather than after the review is already underway.

What to gather before the review starts

  • Prior-year return, if available
  • Current-year income forms such as W-2, 1099, K-1, and business records if applicable
  • Expense support for self-employed work or business activity
  • Any IRS or state notice that may affect timing or filing requirements
  • A short summary of whether this is a personal return, business return, or both

How the preparation process usually works

Most engagements follow a practical structure: gather documents, review completeness, clarify questions, prepare the return, and then complete the final filing or delivery steps. The cleaner the first document package, the smoother each later step becomes.
