Seattle QuickBooks bookkeeping for small businesses: monthly process, cleanup, and what to prepare (2026)

Updated: February 2026

← Back to Updates

If you’re running a small business in the Seattle area, the fastest way to reduce tax‑time stress is consistent monthly bookkeeping—especially if you use QuickBooks Online. This guide explains how the workflow typically looks, what we need from you, and when it makes sense to do a catch‑up/cleanup first.

We’re based in the Seattle area (Seattle / Federal Way) and work remotely across the U.S.—you can share documents electronically and we’ll guide you step by step.

What “healthy books” look like (practical checklist)

  • Bank and credit cards connected (or statements exported monthly)
  • Income categorized correctly (sales vs. owner contributions, etc.)
  • Expenses categorized with notes for unclear items
  • Receipts stored (PDF/photo) for key transactions
  • Reconciliations done monthly

If any of these items are missing, we usually start with a cleanup so future months stay simple.

Monthly bookkeeping workflow in QuickBooks Online

  1. Collect/confirm data sources (bank feeds, statements, sales platforms, payroll reports)
  2. Monthly categorization + review of exceptions
  3. Reconciliation (banks/credit cards)
  4. Basic reporting (P&L / Balance Sheet) and questions back to you

The goal is not “perfect theory”—it’s accurate reporting and clean records for filings.

When you should do catch‑up / cleanup

Catch‑up bookkeeping is common when records were not maintained for several months (or categories are inconsistent). Cleanup is also typical after:

  • Switching accountants/bookkeepers
  • Mixing personal and business spending
  • Adding new revenue channels
  • Payroll started mid‑year

We’ll confirm scope during a short consult and recommend the fastest path.
