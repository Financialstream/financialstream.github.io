If you want cleaner books, calmer month-end reporting, and fewer surprises before tax filing, start with a practical monthly workflow instead of trying to fix everything at year-end.

What to prepare before the first review

  • Your entity type and a short description of the business
  • Whether you already use QuickBooks Online, spreadsheets, or only bank records
  • How many months are behind, if catch-up or cleanup is needed
  • Any payroll, sales-tax, or owner-transaction issues that may affect the books

What good monthly QuickBooks bookkeeping usually includes

A reliable monthly process usually means consistent transaction categorization, reconciliations for bank and credit-card accounts, review of unusual balances, and short monthly reporting that the owner can actually understand. That is what makes the books useful for decisions, not just for compliance.

If categorization changes every month or reconciliations are skipped, the reports may look finished while still being risky to rely on.

When Seattle bookkeeping usually needs cleanup first

Cleanup usually becomes necessary when personal and business transactions are mixed, old uncategorized entries are piling up, duplicates were imported, or prior months were never reconciled. In those cases, monthly bookkeeping and tax prep should not be treated as separate problems.

If you already know that backlog exists, it is better to surface it at the start and connect the work to QuickBooks bookkeeping and tax return preparation from the beginning.
