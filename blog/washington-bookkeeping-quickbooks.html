Clean bookkeeping is the foundation of a calm, compliant business—especially in Washington, where filings and deadlines don’t wait. A consistent monthly close (categorization, reconciliations, and review) keeps your numbers accurate, your QuickBooks reliable, and your decisions grounded in reality.

Top searches we see for Washington businesses

This page is a practical QuickBooks bookkeeping checklist: what to do weekly and monthly, what to review before you file, and how to avoid the most common issues (missing receipts, uncategorized transactions, and reconciliation gaps). Sales tax and other reporting can then be handled as a separate, predictable step once the books are clean.

Checklist: what a clean month looks like

1) Bank/credit card feeds reviewed and categorized consistently.

2) Reconciliations completed (so balances match statements).

3) Liability accounts reviewed (sales tax, payroll, loans).

4) Reports generated: Profit & Loss + Balance Sheet.

Sales tax (state filing) readiness

Sales tax requirements vary by state and by what you sell. Clean bookkeeping makes sales tax filing straightforward because taxable vs non‑taxable revenue is traceable.

Payroll basics

If you run payroll, your bookkeeping should match payroll reports monthly. Small mismatches compound and create year‑end headaches.

Remote workflow

We work remotely with secure document sharing and a simple monthly routine. If you’re in Washington or the Seattle area, time‑zone alignment usually makes communication smoother.
