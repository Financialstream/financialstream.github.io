Washington tax preparation checklist (2026): small business and personal

Searches like tax preparation services Washington, business tax checklist, Washington tax filing help, and tax preparer for LLC usually point to the same situation: filing cannot start cleanly until the records are ready. The right checklist is less about collecting random PDFs and more about making sure the underlying bookkeeping and tax story are clear before review begins.

What to gather first

Start with the prior-year return, current-year income records, and any IRS or state notices that could affect timing. For businesses, this usually also means month-end bookkeeping reports, payroll summaries, owner transaction context, and notes on any one-time events such as a new entity, major purchase, or state change.

What small businesses should review before filing

  • confirm the entity structure and whether anything changed during the year,
  • make sure bookkeeping is closed through the most recent month possible,
  • review balance sheet accounts that still need explanation,
  • check whether payroll, sales tax, or cleanup work could delay the return.

If the books are not current yet, the real task may be tax-readiness first and filing second.

What individuals should review

For personal returns, gather wage forms, 1099s, investment statements, and support for major deductions or credits. If you moved states, changed marital status, added dependents, or had business or self-employment income, flag that early instead of letting it surface halfway through preparation.

What delays filing most often

The usual blockers are not mysterious: late bookkeeping closes, unresolved owner draws, payroll corrections, and notices that were set aside earlier in the year. These are manageable issues, but they slow the process significantly when discovered during filing week.

How to make tax preparation smoother

  1. Collect the core packet first. Prior returns, income forms, notices, and bookkeeping support should come before detailed questions.
  2. Identify exceptions early. Multi-state activity, cleanup work, and payroll issues should be surfaced immediately.
  3. Keep bookkeeping and filing connected. Clean tax prep depends on current records, not just tax software.
