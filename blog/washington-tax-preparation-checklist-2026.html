Core tax packet to gather first

Start with prior-year returns, current-year income statements, owner details, and any IRS or state notices. For businesses, include bookkeeping reports, payroll summaries, and records of major one-time transactions.

Checklist for small business owners

Confirm legal entity structure, ownership changes, state registrations, and whether books are fully reconciled through the most recent month. If there are unresolved balance sheet items, address them before final tax calculations.

Checklist for individual returns

Collect wage and income forms, investment statements, healthcare-related forms, and records supporting major deductions or credits. If you moved states, changed marital status, or added dependents, flag this early.

What delays tax filing most often

Missing bookkeeping closes, unclear owner draws, late payroll corrections, and unaddressed notices are the most common blockers. Fixing these during filing week is possible, but slower and more expensive.

Simple process that reduces filing stress

A practical workflow is: collect documents, verify completeness, resolve exceptions, prepare return, then complete filing/review steps. The more complete the package at step one, the faster each later step moves.
