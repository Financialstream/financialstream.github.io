We prepare and file U.S. tax returns remotely. This guide explains what we typically need, how the process works, and what slows filings down.

Who this is for

  • Individuals and families with U.S. filing obligations
  • Small business owners who want organized documentation
  • Remote clients across the United States (we are based in Washington State)

Documents checklist

  • Basic identity and contact details
  • Income statements and year-end summaries (as applicable)
  • Business records (if you own a business): revenue/expenses summary
  • Prior-year return (helpful, not always required)

How the remote process works

  1. Quick intake: confirm your situation and what you need.
  2. Document collection: you share files securely.
  3. Preparation: we organize, clarify questions, and draft the return.
  4. Review: you confirm key details before filing.

Common mistakes that delay filing

  • Missing documents or incomplete year-end totals
  • Mixing personal and business expenses without separation
  • Unclear multi‑state details when you worked or operated in multiple states

FAQ

Do you work with clients outside Washington?

Yes. We work remotely across the U.S. (based in Washington State).

Do you provide legal advice?

No. We provide accounting and tax preparation support; legal questions should be handled by an attorney.
