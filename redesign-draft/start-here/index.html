What affects the scope and estimate?

Accounting work depends on the current condition of your records, the number of accounts, filing needs, deadlines, and how much cleanup is required. Before work begins, we review the scope so you understand what is included.

  • Transaction volume
  • Number of bank and credit card accounts
  • How far behind the books are
  • Payroll employees or contractors
  • Sales tax obligations
  • Business/entity type
  • Tax filing complexity
  • Deadline urgency

The goal is to define the right scope before starting — not to force a package that does not fit your situation.

Consultation prep

What to prepare before the consultation

You do not need to have everything perfectly organized before reaching out. A few basic details can help us understand your situation faster and suggest the right next step.

  • What service you think you may need
  • Business type or entity
  • State where the business operates
  • Current QuickBooks status
  • Number of bank and credit card accounts
  • Payroll: yes / no
  • Sales tax: yes / no / not sure
  • Any upcoming deadline
  • Your biggest current question

If you do not know some of these answers, that is okay. We can clarify them during the consultation.
