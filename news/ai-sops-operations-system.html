AI SOPs for Operations: Turning Tribal Knowledge into a Working System

Mar 12, 2026 • SOP • Workflow • Quality

Most small teams run on tribal knowledge: “ask Sarah,” “check the inbox,” “it depends.” That becomes expensive when volume grows. The fix is SOP-first design, then controlled AI assistance.

SOP-first design

  • Define branches (service types) and required fields per branch.
  • Specify stop conditions (when a human must step in).
  • Define statuses and ownership (who does what, when).

Where AI adds leverage

  • Classify inbound requests and detect intent.
  • Summarize conversations into structured notes.
  • Draft replies and next-step instructions.

Where AI should not decide

  • Money, refunds, pricing commitments.
  • Legal conclusions and sensitive topics.
  • Any action that changes records without review.

Implementation steps

  • Start with 1–2 high-volume workflows.
  • Add logging and escalation rules.
  • Measure outcomes, then expand to new branches.
